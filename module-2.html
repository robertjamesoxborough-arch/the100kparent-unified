How Salary Sacrifice Works

Salary sacrifice lets you trade salary for pension contributions BEFORE tax. This means you save both Income Tax and National Insurance.

The Magic Formula:

Your employer takes £X from your salary → Puts £X into your pension → You save tax + NI on £X

Example: Sarah earns £95,000

Without salary sacrifice:
Pays 40% tax + 2% NI on £2,000/month childcare = £1,008/month (£12,096/year) after tax

With salary sacrifice:
Sacrifices £2,000/month to pension = Only costs £1,160/month (£13,920/year)
Annual saving: £1,824

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. ❌ Sacrificing too much
    Keep salary above £12,570 to maintain personal allowance
  2. ❌ Forgetting employer match
    Many employers match up to 5% - make sure you get it
  3. ❌ Not combining with Tax-Free Childcare
    Can use BOTH schemes simultaneously
  4. ❌ Ignoring the £100k cliff edge
    If earning £100k+, sacrifice to drop below threshold

Advanced Strategies

Strategy 1: The £100k Sweet Spot
If earning £105k, sacrifice £5k to drop to £100k. This keeps Tax-Free Childcare eligibility (worth £2,000/child) PLUS saves tax/NI on £5k.

Strategy 2: Maximize Employer Match
If employer matches up to 5%, sacrifice at least 5% to get free money. That's an instant 100% return.

Strategy 3: Combine with Bonus Sacrifice
Sacrifice annual bonus to pension. Saves even more tax (especially if bonus pushes you into 45% bracket).

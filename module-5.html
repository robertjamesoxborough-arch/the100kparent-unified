How The Charge Works

The High Income Child Benefit Charge (HICBC) claws back Child Benefit if you or your partner earn over £60,000.

The Rules:

  • Under £60k: Keep full Child Benefit (no charge)
  • £60k-£80k: Pay back 1% for every £100 over £60k
  • Over £80k: Pay back 100% (lose all Child Benefit)

Example: Sarah earns £65,000 with 2 children

Child Benefit: £2,074/year

Over threshold: £65,000 - £60,000 = £5,000

Charge rate: £5,000 ÷ £100 = 50% of benefit

Tax charge: £2,074 × 50% = £1,037

She keeps: £1,037

⚠️ Critical: It's HOUSEHOLD income

The charge applies to the highest earner in the household. If your partner earns £75k and you earn £40k, you'll pay the charge based on £75k.

3 Strategies to Keep Your Benefit

Strategy 1: Salary Sacrifice to £59,999 ✅ BEST

Sacrifice enough salary to pension to drop your adjusted net income below £60,000.

Example: Earn £65k → Sacrifice £5,001 → Stay at £59,999 → Keep full £2,074

Bonus: The £5,001 also saves you tax + NI (£2,100 saved)

Total benefit: £2,074 + £2,100 = £4,174/year

Strategy 2: Gift Aid Donations

Charitable donations reduce your adjusted net income for HICBC purposes.

Example: Earn £62k → Donate £2k → Adjusted income £60k → Keep benefit

Cost: £2,000 donation to keep £2,074 benefit = £74 gain

Strategy 3: Opt Out (if over £80k)

If earning over £80k, opt out of Child Benefit entirely to avoid tax return hassle.

BUT: Still claim it! Just opt out of receiving payment. This protects National Insurance credits.

💡 Why Still Claim Even If Opting Out?

Claiming protects your NI record even if you don't receive payment. This matters for state pension later.

How to Opt Out

If you're over £80k and don't want the admin of claiming then paying back:

  1. Go to: gov.uk/child-benefit-tax-charge
  2. Sign in to your Government Gateway account
  3. Select: "I want to opt out of getting Child Benefit payments"
  4. Confirm - Takes effect from next payment

Important: You still need to file a Self Assessment tax return if you've received ANY Child Benefit payments during the year, even if you opt out mid-year.

Common Mistakes

  1. ❌ Not filing tax return
    HMRC will charge penalties if you don't report the charge
  2. ❌ Using gross income
    It's ADJUSTED net income (after pension contributions, not before)
  3. ❌ Opting out unnecessarily
    If earning £60k-£80k, keep some benefit rather than opt out
  4. ❌ Forgetting NI credits
    Always claim even if opting out of payment
  5. ❌ Not combining with pension
    Salary sacrifice is the most efficient way to stay under £60k

Why Does The £100k Threshold Exist?

The £100,000 threshold was introduced by HMRC as an eligibility limit for means-tested benefits. The logic: high earners don't need government subsidies for childcare.

The Two Benefits You Lose:

1. Tax-Free Childcare (£2,000/child/year)

Government tops up your childcare account by 20%. You deposit £8,000, government adds £2,000 free. BOTH partners must each earn under £100k. If either crosses the threshold, the entire household is ineligible.

2. 30 Hours Free Childcare (£6,840/year)

England-only scheme: 30 hours per week of free nursery for 38 weeks/year (9 months to 4 years old). Worth approximately £6,840/year at average nursery rates. Same rule: BOTH partners under £100k.

The Real Cost

For a family with one child aged 2:

  • Earning £99,999: Gets £8,840/year in benefits
  • Earning £100,001: Gets £0 in benefits
  • £2 extra income costs £8,840 in lost benefits

This is the most brutal cliff edge in the UK tax system.

How Salary Sacrifice Fixes This

Salary sacrifice reduces your adjusted net income — the figure HMRC uses for benefit eligibility.

How It Works:

  1. Current situation: You earn £105,000 gross salary
  2. Action: You salary sacrifice £5,001 into your pension
  3. Result: Your adjusted net income becomes £99,999
  4. Outcome: You're now eligible for Tax-Free Childcare + 30 Hours Free

The Triple Win:

Win 1: You save tax + NI on the £5,001 sacrificed (£2,100 at 42% rate)

Win 2: You unlock £8,840/year in childcare benefits

Win 3: The sacrificed money still belongs to you — it's in your pension

The Maths:

  • Sacrifice: £5,001
  • Tax + NI saved: £2,100
  • Benefits unlocked: £8,840
  • Total gain: £10,940/year
  • Net cost to you: £2,901 (goes into pension)

💡 Key insight: You're "spending" £2,901 to get £8,840 in benefits. That's a 304% return on investment.

Step-by-Step: How To Implement

Week 1: Calculate and Request

  1. Use the calculator above to determine your exact sacrifice amount
  2. Email HR requesting salary sacrifice setup (template in Module 2)
  3. Specify the exact monthly sacrifice amount (annual amount ÷ 12)

Week 2: Sign and Confirm

  1. HR will send you a new salary agreement to sign
  2. Review carefully: ensure the gross salary reduction is correct
  3. Ask payroll to confirm your new adjusted net income will be under £100k
  4. Request written confirmation of the change

Week 3: Apply for Benefits

  1. Wait for your first payslip showing the reduced salary
  2. Apply for Tax-Free Childcare at childcarechoices.gov.uk
  3. Apply for 30 Hours Free at the same site
  4. Both applications ask for your income — use your NEW reduced figure

Week 4: Set Reminders

  1. Tax-Free Childcare requires reconfirmation every 3 months
  2. Set a recurring calendar reminder
  3. Missing a reconfirmation suspends your account immediately

⚠️ Critical: Don't apply for benefits BEFORE your salary is reduced. HMRC will check your current income and reject you. Wait for the payslip.

Common Questions

What if my partner earns over £100k too?

Both partners need to salary sacrifice below £100k. If Partner 1 earns £105k and Partner 2 earns £110k, BOTH need to sacrifice. Partner 1 sacrifices £5,001, Partner 2 sacrifices £10,001.

Can I salary sacrifice other things instead of pension?

For the purpose of reducing adjusted net income, pension contributions are the most effective. Cycle-to-work and electric car schemes also reduce income but are capped at lower amounts.

What if I get a pay rise next year?

You'll need to increase your sacrifice amount to stay under £100k. Review every January and adjust with HR.

Does this work if I'm self-employed?

Self-employed people can make pension contributions that reduce adjusted net income for benefit eligibility. However, "salary sacrifice" specifically is an employment mechanism. See Module 4 for self-employed strategies.

What if my income varies month-to-month?

HMRC looks at your annual income when you apply for benefits. If you're close to £100k, it's safer to sacrifice enough to create a buffer (e.g. drop to £97k instead of £99,999).

How Income Splitting Works

Income splitting moves income from a higher-earning partner to a lower-earning partner to use their unused tax allowances and lower tax bands.

For Company Directors:

  1. Make partner a shareholder (issue shares via Companies House)
  2. Pay yourself minimum salary (£12,570/year)
  3. Pay partner dividend income from company profits
  4. Partner pays tax at their lower rate (0% or 8.75% vs your 33.75% or 39.35%)

Example: Tom & Sarah (Directors)

Before:
Tom: £95k (40% tax bracket)
Sarah: £25k (20% tax bracket)
Combined tax: £26,540

After splitting:
Tom: £70k salary
Sarah: £25k salary + £25k dividends
Combined tax: £23,165
Annual saving: £3,375

Step-by-Step Setup

Method 1: Share Issuance (Company Directors)

  1. Issue shares to partner (1 hour)
    - Hold board meeting
    - File Form SH01 with Companies House
    - Update Companies House within 14 days
  2. Set up dividend policy (30 min)
    - Decide dividend split (e.g. 50/50)
    - Document in board minutes
    - Set regular payment schedule
  3. Pay dividends quarterly (15 min each)
    - Hold board meeting
    - Issue dividend vouchers
    - Transfer funds
    - Record in accounts

⚠️ HMRC Will Check:

"Genuine commercial benefit" - Partner must actually work in the business. Document their role and hours.

"Settlements legislation" - Don't give shares as a gift then pay all dividends to them. Must be proportional to shareholding.

Common Mistakes

  1. ❌ Giving all shares to lower earner
    HMRC will challenge. Keep it proportional (max 50/50).
  2. ❌ Not documenting partner's work
    Keep timesheets, emails, meeting notes proving they work in business.
  3. ❌ Forgetting dividend tax
    Dividends aren't tax-free. Partner pays 8.75% (basic) or 33.75% (higher).
  4. ❌ Mixing with Tax-Free Childcare
    Dividend income counts toward £100k limit.

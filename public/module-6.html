How Junior ISAs Work

A Junior ISA is a tax-free savings account for under-18s. Any growth is completely tax-free, forever.

The Rules:

  • Who can open: Parent or guardian
  • Annual limit: £9,000 (2025/26)
  • Access: Child gets control at 16, withdraws at 18
  • Tax: 0% on growth, 0% on dividends, 0% on gains
  • Contribution: Anyone can pay in (parents, grandparents, friends)

Types of Junior ISA:

Cash Junior ISA: Like a savings account. Current rates: 4-5%. Safe but lower returns.

Stocks & Shares Junior ISA: Invest in funds. Historical average: 7%. Higher risk, higher returns over 18 years.

Recommendation for 18-year timescale: Stocks & Shares (time smooths out volatility)

Alternative: Bare Trusts

A bare trust lets you invest MORE than £9,000/year, but with tax implications.

How It Works:

  • You open investment account in child's name
  • No annual limit (invest £20k, £50k, £100k+)
  • Child owns assets but you control until 18
  • Use child's £3,000 CGT allowance + £1,000 dividend allowance

Tax Treatment:

  • First £1,000 dividends: Tax-free
  • £3,000 capital gains: Tax-free per year
  • Above that: Child pays tax (at child's rate, usually 0%)

⚠️ Warning: Parental Settlement Rules

If YOU gift money and it generates income over £100/year, that income is taxed as YOURS, not child's.

Solution: Grandparents or other relatives gift instead (rules don't apply to them)

Common Mistakes

  1. ❌ Choosing cash over stocks for 18-year timeframe
    Stocks historically outperform cash over 15+ years
  2. ❌ High-fee platforms
    1% fee vs 0.25% = £3,000 less at age 18
  3. ❌ Not starting early
    Starting at birth vs age 10 = £26k difference
  4. ❌ Stopping contributions in market crashes
    Crashes = buying opportunity. Keep contributing.
  5. ❌ Picking individual stocks
    Use diversified funds. Don't gamble on Apple or Tesla.

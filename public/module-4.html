What You Can Claim

As a self-employed parent, you can deduct "wholly and exclusively" business expenses from your taxable profit.

✅ Always Claimable:

  • Home office - Proportion of rent, utilities, council tax
  • Business mileage - 45p/mile (first 10k miles), 25p after
  • Equipment - Laptop, phone, furniture, software
  • Professional fees - Accountant, solicitor, industry memberships
  • Marketing - Website, advertising, business cards
  • Training - Courses, books, conferences

⚠️ Partially Claimable:

  • Mobile phone - Business use % only
  • Internet - Business use % (usually 50%)
  • Car expenses - Choose mileage OR actual costs (not both)

❌ NOT Claimable:

  • Childcare - Not tax-deductible (use Tax-Free Childcare instead)
  • Commuting - Travel from home to regular workplace
  • Personal expenses - Anything with dual purpose must be split
  • Entertainment - Client meals/drinks (changed in 2016)

Record Keeping

HMRC requires 6 years of records. Keep:

  1. Every receipt - Paper or digital (take photos)
  2. Bank statements - Business account mandatory for £100k+ earners
  3. Invoices - All sales and purchases
  4. Mileage log - Date, destination, miles, purpose

💡 Recommended Apps:

  • Receipts: Expensify, Receipt Bank, Dext
  • Mileage: MileIQ, Driversnote
  • Accounting: Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent

Common Mistakes

  1. ❌ No separate bank account
    HMRC expects business/personal separation
  2. ❌ Claiming 100% of phone/internet
    Be realistic - 50% business use is typical
  3. ❌ No mileage log
    Without records, HMRC will disallow the claim
  4. ❌ Guessing proportions
    Home office % must be accurate (measure rooms)
  5. ❌ Mixing personal & business
    Avoid claiming gym membership as "stress relief"

Most Valuable Benefits

1. Cycle to Work (£200-£500/year)

Buy bike through salary sacrifice. Save 25-40% on retail price.

How it works: Employer buys bike → You lease via salary sacrifice → Saves income tax + NI

Example: £1,500 bike costs you £1,000 after tax savings

2. Electric Car Scheme (£300-£2,000/year)

Lease electric car through salary sacrifice. Massive tax break (2% benefit-in-kind for EVs).

Example: £40k Tesla Model 3 = £67/month benefit-in-kind tax vs £400/month for petrol equivalent

3. Childcare Vouchers (£600/year - Legacy)

If enrolled before October 2018, keep using! Don't switch to Tax-Free Childcare (vouchers are better).

Saving: £243/month tax-free = £600/year saving vs paying after-tax

4. Additional Pension Match (£250+/year)

Negotiate higher employer contribution. Many employers will match up to 10%.

Example: Increase from 5% to 8% match = £2,850 extra into pension (on £95k salary)

How to Negotiate

Step 1: Research What's Available

  1. Check employee handbook
  2. Ask HR what flex benefits exist
  3. Look at job ads for competitors (what do they offer?)

Step 2: Build Business Case

Use employer NI calculator above to show cost savings

Key message: "This saves you money while improving employee retention"

Step 3: Email HR

📧 Template Email:

Subject: Flexible Benefits Request

Hi [HR Manager],

I'd like to discuss adding flexible benefits to my package.

I'm interested in:
- Cycle to Work scheme (£1,500 bike)
- Increased pension match (5% → 8%)

This would cost the company approximately £[X] after NI savings, 
while providing me £[Y] value - a win/win.

Could we discuss this in my next review?

Thanks,
[Your name]

Step 4: Time It Right

  • Best time: Annual review, promotion, job offer stage
  • Avoid: Company cost-cutting periods, immediately after payrise
  • Leverage: Competing job offers, retention concerns

Common Mistakes

  1. ❌ Not asking
    Many employers offer benefits but don't advertise them
  2. ❌ Asking for everything at once
    Pick 1-2 benefits max, show value, then expand later
  3. ❌ Forgetting childcare vouchers if already enrolled
    DON'T switch to Tax-Free Childcare (vouchers are better)
  4. ❌ Not showing employer benefit
    Always frame as "saves you £X in NI"
  5. ❌ Treating as entitlement
    Frame as "mutual benefit" not "I deserve this"
